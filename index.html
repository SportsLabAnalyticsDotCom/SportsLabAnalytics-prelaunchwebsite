Largest sponsorship deals in soccer

A quick look at the biggest sponsorship deals in soccer, the commercial power of global events, and why sponsorship is now about far more than logo placement.

JF
Joao Freitas
Contributor
March 3rd 2026 · Less than a minute

Introduction

As a marketing student, I’m naturally interested in viewing sports not only as a form of entertainment, but also as a global business system. I’m also interested in the way partnerships work and how they add value to both sides.

How companies get global visibility and positioning, how teams get financial security and grow into the future.

The sponsorship market is estimated at a figure above $8B by 2032. Football is a significant driver of this market.

Some of the largest sponsorship deals for football clubs

  • Puma and Manchester City F.C. – £1B+ over 10 years
  • Nike and FC Barcelona – ~€1.7B over 13 years
  • Emirates and Real Madrid C.F. – €70M per year
  • Spotify and FC Barcelona – ~€70M per year
  • Qatar Airways and Paris Saint Germain F.C. – ~€70M per year
  • Qualcomm Snapdragon and Manchester United F.C. – ~£70M per year

These deals are about long-term security for clubs and enduring visibility for brands, creating partnerships that extend far beyond the shirt.

The power of the athletes

Nike has three of the largest sponsorship deals with athletes:

  • Michael Jordan – ~$1.3B
  • Cristiano Ronaldo – ~$1B
  • LeBron James – ~$1B

In these cases, the athletes themselves become global platforms. The relationship between brand and player is about long-term cultural relevance as much as it is about short-term sales.

The Global Event Multiplier

While the biggest commercial spikes may not always occur at club level, they certainly do at global events.

The FIFA World Cup may arguably have the strongest sponsorship platform in sports. Global sponsors like Coca-Cola and Visa invest heavily for multi-cycle rights to the World Cup because of the sheer scale of the audience and exposure, particularly with the upcoming World Cup in North America in 2026.

The Olympic Games have an exclusive model for their TOP partner sponsors, and global sponsors like Samsung and Toyota have invested heavily in this platform. While this may not have the immediate commercial spike of other events, sponsorship for the Olympics is long-term and about global positioning and brand trust.

Modern sponsorship is more than logos

On top of the headline deals, there is a wider ecosystem shaping the sponsorship landscape:

  • 66% of clubs across 31 divisions have a betting deal
  • £135M spent on Premier League shirt deals by betting brands alone
  • Clubs increasingly looking for multi-year deals for greater stability

Sponsorship today is no longer just about logo placement. It is about:

  • Strategic global positioning
  • Cultural relevance
  • Long-term brand equity for both the brand and the club

Newcastle Investment

Newcastle United wasn't a risky investment in 2021. It was an undervalued one.

JF
Joao Freitas
Contributor
March 1st 2026 · 1 minute

Introduction

Newcastle is one of the most valuable teams in the World, and it is not only because of money.

Founded in 1892, the club has always had one thing investors look for first: Predictable demand.

2016/17 – Championship Performance

Playing in the Championship after relegation, Newcastle demonstrated remarkable resilience:

  • 51,108 average attendance - 97% stadium capacity
  • Highest Championship income (£85.7m) - £10m more than 2nd place
  • £23.4m matchday Income - £12.7m more than 2nd place

That's Premier League-level revenue… in the second division.

2021/22 – Post-Acquisition Stability

After the PIF acquisition (£305m for 80%), the numbers show incredible stability:

  • 51,487 average attendance
  • Only a 0.74% increase

Promotion, relegation, ownership change — attendance barely moved. The fanbase in North East England was already elite. The asset was stable.

Financial Performance

The club's financial health was strong even before new investment:

  • Profitable 4 of the previous 5 seasons (£115.8m total profit)
  • One £19m loss taken strategically - Championship promotion strategy
  • Controlled wage structure
  • Strong matchday consistency

This wasn't a distressed brand. It was an under-optimized one.

Investment Impact

With investment:

  • ~£450m injected into the squad
  • 71 points in 2022/23
  • 4th place
  • Champions League football
  • Amazing players + emerging talents

Current Position

Now:

  • 52,187 average attendance
  • European presence
  • Global commercial growth

The Investment Lesson

Strong fan base + strong investment = success.

Investors don't buy trophies. They buy predictable demand with growth potential. Newcastle already had the first.

NBA cancels Atlanta Hawks' planned Magic City promotion

The NBA has stepped in and cancelled the Atlanta Hawks' plans for a Magic City promotion night on March 16.

KH
Kurt Helin
Contributor
March 9th 2026 · 1 minute

The Controversial Promotion

The Hawks described Magic City as an "iconic cultural institution" — and it is that, having been around for 40 years — but Magic City is better known as a legendary strip club, one that has been central to the city's hip-hop scene for decades.

NBA's Intervention

"When we became aware of the Atlanta Hawks' scheduled promotion, we reached out to Hawks leadership to better understand their plans and rationale," NBA Commissioner Adam Silver said. "While we appreciate the team's perspective and their desire to move forward, we have heard significant concerns from a broad array of league stakeholders, including fans, partners and employees. I believe canceling this promotion is the right decision for the broader NBA community."

Hawks' Response

"While we are very disappointed in the NBA's decision to cancel our Magic City Night promotion, we fully respect their decision," the Hawks said in a statement. "As a franchise, we remain committed to celebrating the best of Atlanta — with authenticity — in ways that continue to unite and bring us all together."

What Atlanta Wanted to Celebrate

What Atlanta wanted to celebrate was the unity that the Magic City brings to some in Atlanta. It is a place where T.I., Migos, Jermaine Dupri, 2 Chainz, Killer Mike, Big Boi, Future and many others got a big break by having their music played at the club. It is also famed for its lemon-pepper wings, which were going to be served at the game.

However, there was pushback from some players publicly, and other league stakeholders privately.

What's Still Happening

Some of that will still be available at the March 16 game, including wings, the team announced. Also, Legendary rapper and 'King of the South' T.I. will perform at halftime as previously scheduled.

Ravens Nix Trade For Raiders OLB Maxx Crosby

The Maxx Crosby deal is off, according to the Raiders.

SR
Sam Robinson
Contributor
March 10th 2026 · 1 minute

Trade Deal Falls Through

The Baltimore Ravens and Las Vegas Raiders had been discussing a potential trade for star outside linebacker Maxx Crosby, but those talks have now ended. According to sources close to the Raiders organization, the deal is officially off.

Crosby's Value to the Raiders

Maxx Crosby has been one of the Raiders' most consistent defensive players since being selected in the fourth round of the 2019 NFL Draft. The edge rusher has developed into a Pro Bowl caliber player and has been a cornerstone of the Raiders' defensive unit.

Ravens' Defensive Needs

The Ravens were reportedly interested in adding Crosby to bolster their pass rush, which has been an area of focus for the team as they look to remain competitive in the AFC North. However, the two sides were unable to come to terms on compensation.

What This Means Moving Forward

With the trade discussions concluded, Crosby will remain with the Raiders for the foreseeable future. The Ravens will need to look elsewhere to address their defensive needs, potentially through free agency or the NFL Draft.

Tennis news: Novak Djokovic 'not happy' as Emma Raducanu sent home from Indian Wells

A Recap of the latest news from the Indian Wells Open as it continues to take centre stage in the United States.

SK
Stephen Killen, Patrick Austen-Hardy, Matthew Abbott
Contributors
March 10th 2026 · 1 minute

Third Round Shocks at Indian Wells

The third round saw a series of shocks as Emma Raducanu and Coco Gauff bowed out at Indian Wells. The British no.1 was beaten in just 52 minutes while the American retired in the second set through injury.

Alcaraz Breaks Silence on Shot Clock Controversy

Carlos Alcaraz has broken his silence after the shot clocker controversy in Qatar but refused to be drawn on the flashpoint after progressing in California.

Djokovic and British Stars in Action

Novak Djokovic is in action as well as British duo Cameron Norrie and Jack Draper who take on Alex de Minaur and Francisco Cerundolo respectively.

Tournament Spotlight

The Indian Wells Open continues to take centre stage in the United States, attracting attention from fans and media worldwide. The tournament's prestige and challenging conditions make it a key event in the tennis calendar.

Broader Tournament Context

As the tournament progresses, players are adapting to various challenges including weather conditions, scheduling, and the high expectations that come with competing at one of tennis's premier events outside of the Grand Slams.
