AI companies are rapidly becoming a new category of global partners across multiple sports

Something interesting is happening in sports sponsorship right now: AI companies are rapidly becoming a new category of global partners across multiple sports.

At the Formula 1 Australian Grand Prix alone, several leading AI players were visible across the grid:

  • Perplexity → Lewis Hamilton
  • Google Gemini → McLaren Racing
  • Anthropic (Claude) → Williams Racing
  • AI at Meta → Mercedes-AMG PETRONAS Formula One Team
  • CoreWeave → Aston Martin F1 Team

But this trend isn't restricted to Formula 1.

In football, Google Gemini has signed a global partnership with the Asociación del Fútbol Argentino. SAS partnered with Liverpool Football Club, Perplexity with Ronaldo, and more.

In cricket, Google is working with the International Cricket Council to power AI-driven fan experiences for the 2026 Men's T20 World Cup. ChatGPT partnered with WPL.

We have seen the same in tennis too, via Infosys, IBM, and Harvey.

Taken together, this points to a broader shift: AI companies are beginning to treat sport as a strategic go-to-market platform.

Three dynamics are driving this:

1️⃣ Technology storytelling

Sport provides a powerful environment to demonstrate themes that AI companies want to own — performance, decision-making, optimisation, and speed.

2️⃣ Global distribution at scale

Very few platforms combine global reach, cultural relevance, and live audiences the way major sports properties do.

3️⃣ Innovation testbeds

From fan engagement tools to data platforms and content workflows, sport offers a real-time environment to deploy and showcase AI applications.

What's particularly interesting is how different sports are emerging as different AI narratives:

  • Formula 1 → engineering, simulation, high-performance data
  • Football → global consumer reach and digital engagement
  • Cricket → large-scale fan platforms and emerging markets

We've seen sponsorship cycles driven by categories before — airlines, fintech, crypto. The AI wave feels different.

This is less about a marketing cycle and more about a technology shift that will increasingly shape how sport is produced, consumed, and commercialised.
