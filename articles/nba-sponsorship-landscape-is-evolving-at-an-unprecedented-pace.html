The NBA's sponsorship landscape is evolving at an unprecedented pace

The NBA's sponsorship surge is not just about more deals. It reflects a more integrated, content-driven commercial model built around multi-asset partnerships, premium visibility, and digital engagement.

Anubhav Roda

The NBA's sponsorship landscape is evolving at an unprecedented pace.

For the 2024–25 season, NBA teams generated a record $1.62 billion in sponsorship revenue, an 8% year-on-year jump and an astonishing 91% increase over the past five years. This surge isn't just about more deals; it's about smarter, more integrated partnerships.

What's driving this growth?

What's driving this growth?

💥 Multi-Asset Partnerships: NBA teams are moving beyond volume, focusing on deeper, multi-channel deals that span broadcast, digital, in-venue, and community activations. The number of assets sold per deal is at a five-year high, reflecting brands' confidence in the NBA's global reach and engaged fanbase.

💥 Jersey Patch Boom: The number of jersey patch deals more than doubled year-over-year. These high-visibility assets alone generated over $80 million in new investments, with 55% of new patch sponsors being first-time NBA partners.

💥 Sector Shifts: Technology, automotive, and healthcare brands are leading the charge, favoring prominent assets like jersey patches and naming rights. Notable deals include Intuit's naming rights for LA Clippers' new Intuit Dome and expanded partnerships from Toyota Motor Corporation and Kaiser Permanente.

💥 Player-Driven Content: NBA athletes are now powerful content creators. Players like Jared McCain are reshaping endorsement strategies, with brands increasingly valuing digital influence and social engagement alongside on-court performance.

💥 New Entrants & Local Focus: Nearly 450 brands joined the NBA sponsorship ecosystem this season, with growth from construction, alcohol, and tech sectors. There's a notable trend toward local marketing, especially among government and hospitality sponsors.

As the NBA prepares for its landmark $76 billion media rights deal next season, the league's commercial platform is only set to become more dynamic.

For brands, the message is clear: the NBA's integrated, content-driven sponsorship model offers unmatched opportunities for engagement and growth.
